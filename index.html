Do I Care

Exhibit J

No, I don't think so. It's just when I think of life without you, my brain boils over like the blue-lit LED kettle we watched from the wooden floor. The tea's gone cold, and a wave of blue rolls over me differently. Fearful avoidant, maybe — but I'm all out of fear for you now. The only thing I'd like to avoid is going to sleep and waking again without hearing your lisp.

Multicoloured lights bring back multicoloured nights with you. One rascal to another. Two tabs, two lips, one heart, one kiss. Eyes closed, bright hearts fell from the sky like rain.
Two plants in the desert, nourished by the love falling. A hallucination befitting my illuminated lover. How lucky I was to be so close to a warmth of your magnitude. And how lucky the world is that you move through it at all, leaving laughter, light, and kindness in your wake.

Do I care? No, it's just I'm often taken back to the moment your feet left the floor to do the splits and point at me, like gravity chose to be gentle with you. When I see a falling star, I wonder if it's trying to get closer to you too.

Memories of you are burned into my optic nerve like an old TV left on the same channel too long.
I look into a green, mossy stream and see your eyes stare softly back.
If only it were true. How I wish I sat there with you. All the artefacts of our love, pieces of me and you, clues and treasure, like breadcrumbs leading me back to a part of myself I thought was lost, and hopefully back to you.

Do I care? No, I just wish I could peel you one more orange and hear about your day. The same way I wish I could peel back time and carry the weight of whatever you laid before me under my fingernails. I wish I could still be somewhere your shoulders softened, somewhere your heart could rest. Your laugh echoes through my subconscious in valleys long forgotten. You make me feel electric. Every glance from you brews a storm where fear falls, hope rises, and the air between us breaks into sparks.

You are the scientist — I'm sure you've always felt the chemistry. I'd still like to experiment. My heart will simply have no other. If fear is the mind-killer, then hope is the great liberator. So, when it comes to you, I choose hope.

Do I care? No, I'd just like to thank you for being you, for loving me, for existing as yourself.
For the way you see things deeply. For the way you make ordinary moments glow. For being soft where the world is hard. I hope we can start over fresh. Maybe return to those two shy kids too timid to ask each other to the bedroom, who fell asleep on the couch together.

I'd like to keep doing Irish goodbyes and climbing over fences with my spéirbhean.

Maybe then I'll get to kiss you again, just to show you how your name is always
on the tip of my tongue.

Care about you? I'm hoping you know now.
Of course I do.
